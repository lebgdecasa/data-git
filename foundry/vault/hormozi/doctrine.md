# Hormozi Doctrine — Operating Laws

Distilled from the owner's uploaded sources (see sibling files) plus the published
$100M Offers / $100M Leads frameworks. This is the board's default playbook for
offers, pricing, lead generation, and scaling.

## The Master Sequence

**Get Flow → Monetize Flow → THEN Add Friction.**
Generate demand first. Figure out monetization second. Optimize/filter last.
Most founders invert this and die optimizing a funnel nobody enters.

## Offer Laws

1. **The Grand Slam Offer comes first.** Promotions (free/discount) are wrapping paper; the gift inside must already be irresistible. Value equation: (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice).
2. **Specificity gives copy its edge.** Not "working hard" — "cleaning the bathrooms yet again." Know the avatar's actual Tuesday.
3. **An offer too good to be true fails on believability.** Every crazy offer must answer "why are you doing this?"

## Pricing Laws

4. **There is nothing below $0, but there is no ceiling above.** Premium pricing has infinite upside; one $10k sale ≈ 9.5x the profit of 190 × $100 sales, with 5% the operational load.
5. **If lead costs are 5–10x higher (premium), prices must be at least 5–10x higher.** Mixing free-offer prices with premium-offer acquisition is the #1 documented mistake. Price, Prospect, Process, Promotion, Product must all match ONE strategy.
6. **3–5x price increases on existing services are usually possible** once the offer is restructured ($100M Offers mechanics).

## Free & Discount Laws

7. **The Penny Gap:** 9x more people take a free thing than a $0.01 thing. Free is categorically different, not just cheaper.
8. **"Free brings broke people" is a myth:** 4 independent split tests — close rates and ticket sizes were IDENTICAL between free and non-free respondents; free leads simply cost ~5x less.
9. **Free is only free for the client.** Hormozi's first $1M outside gyms: "I fly out, I spend my own money, I work your leads, I keep the cash I collect." Results-in-advance, ruthlessly monetized. Design the upsell/testimonial/referral mechanics BEFORE giving anything away.
10. **Discounts must be massive (50%+) to drive behavior.** 5–25% off just burns margin. Discount a *splinter* of the offer, never the core (or customers learn to wait for sales).
11. **Discounts shine in regulated industries** (where "free" claims are restricted) **and well-understood services** (people know the reference price).

## Sales Laws

12. **Two-step sale:** cheap/discounted front-end → deliver value → card on file → premium upsell. "You'd be amazed how many more people buy when they don't have to take their card out."
13. **Paid front-ends kill no-shows** (~85–90%+ show rates when money changed hands). Alternative: charge a no-show fee on the card from step one.
14. **Friction levers** (each cuts volume, raises quality): qualifications in copy · more/heavier form fields · more steps · forced consumption of sales material before CTA · longer ads/videos. Find the sweet spot: "weed out the weirdos without losing the lazy whales."
15. **The information advantage:** you know the customer's problem-journey better than they do. Make them aware of the problems they'll hit next — that's the ethical engine of upsells.

## Lead Generation (published $100M Leads, board's base knowledge)

16. **Core Four:** warm outreach, content, cold outreach, paid ads — in roughly that order for a new founder.
17. **Rule of 100:** 100 primary actions per day (100 cold contacts, or 100 min of content, or $100 of ads) for 100 days before judging a channel.
18. **Lead getters multiply you:** referrals, employees, agencies, affiliates.

## Scaling Laws (Leila — activate when team exists)

19. Customers leave from bad service more than bad product or price. Five Star standard: Concern, Courtesy, One & Done, Educate & Empower, Timeliness — measured by post-interaction surveys.
20. Departments fail in silos: weekly Gametape Review (Marketing+Sales+CS watch one real call) — fix ONE "big domino" per week, not ten things.
21. Communication predicts team output better than individual talent: maximize Energy, Engagement, Exploration.
22. One hour of weekly planning (Monday Hour One) saves 10–12 hours of drift.
23. Transparent pay bands prevent losing A-players: 1–3% standard · 4–5% reliable · 5–10% excellence · 10–20% promotion/new scope.
