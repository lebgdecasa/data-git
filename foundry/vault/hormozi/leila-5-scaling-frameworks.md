# Leila Hormozi — 5 Scaling Frameworks (Acquisition.com, 2024)

Source: owner-uploaded PDF, full text. Internal SOPs Leila uses across the Acquisition.com
portfolio (~$500M). **Stage-gated: these activate when a team exists** — except Monday Hour One,
which the owner runs solo from day one.

## 1. Five Star Service Framework (customer support standard)

Why: customers leave from bad service more than bad price/product (McKinsey). Their NPS: 88
(Chick-fil-A 58, Apple 61).

The five standards, with scripted behaviors:
1. **Concern** — thank + personalize, reassure ("happy to resolve X today"), verify the issue, confirm resolution at the end.
2. **Courtesy** — active listening (rephrase their issue back), probing questions to the root, rapport (name, small talk), please/thank-you, acknowledge gratitude.
3. **One & Done** — "no leftovers": resolve fully the first time, summarize steps taken, prevent recurrence, offer additional help before closing.
4. **Educate & Empower** — answer FIRST, then show where the answer lives, then explain the cause so they can self-serve next time.
5. **Timeliness** — acknowledge immediately even if you can't solve immediately; one inquiry at a time to full resolution.

Measurement: 5-question post-interaction survey, each standard rated 1–5, plus an overall
five-star rating. "Deploy without measuring and you will fail."

## 2. Gametape Review Framework (cross-team call review)

Why: departments operate in silos; the customer doesn't. Leila still attends these at $500M —
it's her go-to diagnostic when any revenue department underperforms.

The 5 A's:
1. **Arrange** — recurring weekly 60–90 min. Host: Head of Sales. Attendees: heads of Marketing, Sales, CS (+ anyone who'd benefit).
2. **Assemble** — day before, pick ONE call: sales, onboarding, or service. Rotate normally; if one department is struggling, review only its calls for 4 straight weeks.
3. **Assess** — watch together at 1.5x. Each lens has a job: Sales → what went right / what to improve. Marketing → which objections could ads/content kill before the call; which can't (sales must train on those). CS → what must be handed to delivery; what was promised that we can't deliver (sales script must change).
4. **Analyze** — everyone shares; commit to ONE action: the "big domino." Picking many things breaks execution bandwidth.
5. **Act** — document, monitor, adjust next week.

## 3. High-Performance Communication Framework

Why: MIT badge study across industries — communication patterns predicted team success better
than individual intelligence or skill. Energy + engagement explained 40% of variance in
dollar productivity.

The 3 E's and how to raise each:
- **Energy** (number/nature of interactions): be a FAST responder · "if you see something, say something" — daily end-of-day praise block · vary mediums (chat, reactions, comments, gifts).
- **Engagement** (distribution of energy): pull quiet people in (private-chat them lines to say until confident) · rotate who presents each meeting · buddy up teammates who rarely interact.
- **Exploration** (cross-team communication — the multiplier): cross-department special projects · monthly sit-ins on other teams' calls · casual events; annual meetups with deliberately mixed seating.

Deploy: leaders grade their team 1–10 on each E; the team itself picks ONE area to fix.

## 4. Monday Hour One (weekly planning — ACTIVE NOW, team of one)

One hour of planning saves 10–12 hours/week. Manager (or future you) should see the whole
week's allocation at a glance.

1. **Look Back** — last week's calendar: undone items? Sent emails: unanswered threads? Missed messages? Last week's constraint, and what process prevents it recurring?
2. **Look Forward** — next 1–3 months: projects to start, events to prepare, quarterly goals; what must be pulled into THIS week?
3. **Prioritize** — schedule other stuff around priorities, never priorities around other stuff.
4. **Schedule** — named blocks ("Project X build", never generic "project work"), realistic (lunch, breaks), email/admin in designated slots.
5. **Overflow/Ad-hoc** — explicit buffer blocks; constraints make work compress.
6. **Color code** — recurring meetings blue · admin red · one-off meetings yellow · priorities/projects green · breaks purple · personal orange.

## 5. Pay Increase Framework (compensation philosophy)

Why: without a pay philosophy you overpay bad people and underpay good ones — and people TALK.
Transparency kills drama.

Raise bands by performance category:
- Standard / not meeting criteria: **1–3%**
- Consistent + reliable: **4–5%**
- Excellence / added responsibilities: **5–10%**
- Promotion / new skills / major scope increase: **10–20%**

Bigger raises come from: documented scope increase (JD changes), promotion to leadership,
sustained over-delivery (ICs rewarded no less than leaders). Cadence: case-by-case, max ~1×/year
absent promotion. Asking well: market data (Payscale/Glassdoor) + quantified contributions,
"lead with data, not emotions" — best requests arrive as a slide deck.

## Tying it together

These five exist to keep delivery, alignment, communication, focus, and compensation from
breaking as headcount grows. The board's rule: don't deploy team SOPs on a team of zero —
but the moment the owner hires (VA, setter, contractor), pull this file back up.
